**CONSTITUTION OF TSIRELEDZO CARE**

**1. Name**
1.1 The organisation hereby constituted will be called TSIRELEDZO CARE.
1.2 Its shortened name will be TSIRELEDZO CARE (hereinafter referred to as the "organisation").
1.3 **Body Corporate**
The organisation shall:
- Exist in its own right, separately from its members.
- Continue to exist even when its membership changes and there are different office bearers.
- Be able to own property and other possessions.
- Be able to sue and be sued in its own name.

**2. Objectives**
2.1 **Main Objectives**
The organisation’s main objectives are to:
- Address the phenomenon of bullying among learners in the school setting by providing social worker intervention.
- Educate learners identified as bullying perpetrators and assist them in developing new, socially acceptable behaviors and skills through life skills education including conflict resolution, critical thinking, self-awareness, and problem solving.

2.2 **Secondary Objectives**
The organisation’s secondary objectives are to:
- Collaborate with technology companies to insource youth from our region for projects, particularly in the fiber space industry, thereby creating job opportunities and training programs that can help reduce unemployment, crime, and alcohol abuse.

**3. Income and Property**
3.1 The organisation will keep a record of everything it owns.
3.2 The organisation may not distribute any of its money or property to its members or office bearers except as payment for work performed for the organisation. Such payments must be reasonable for the work done.
3.3 Members can only be reimbursed for expenses paid on behalf of the organisation.
3.4 Members and office bearers do not have rights over the organisation’s property.

**4. Membership and General Meetings**
4.1 Prospective members must apply to the management committee for membership. The management committee reserves the right to reject applications.
4.2 Members are required to attend the organisation’s annual general meetings (AGMs) where they will participate in determining the organisation’s policies.

**5. Management**
5.1 The organisation will be managed by a management committee consisting of no fewer than six (6) members, who will serve as office bearers.
5.2 Office bearers will serve for a term of one year but can stand for re-election. They may serve multiple terms if their services are needed and they are willing.
5.3 If a committee member misses three consecutive meetings without leave, the committee may replace that member.
5.4 The management committee will meet at least once a month. A quorum requires the presence of more than half of the members.
5.5 Minutes will be recorded for each meeting, distributed to members two weeks before the next meeting, and signed by the chairperson after confirmation.
5.6 The organisation may form sub-committees whose decisions must be ratified by the management committee.
5.7 All members must adhere to decisions made by the management committee.

**6. Powers of the Organisation**
6.1 The management committee has the authority to raise funds, accept contributions, and make decisions necessary to achieve the organisation’s objectives.
6.2 The committee may acquire, hire, or exchange property necessary for its operations.
6.3 The committee may establish by-laws for the proper management of the organisation, including membership procedures.
6.4 The organisation will define the powers and functions of its office bearers.

**7. Meetings and Procedures of the Committee**
7.1 The management committee will hold at least two ordinary meetings each year.
7.2 Special meetings may be called by the chairperson or two committee members with a minimum of 21 days’ notice. For matters involving new committee members, 30 days' notice is required.
7.3 The chairperson will preside over meetings. In their absence, a chairperson will be elected from among the present members.
7.4 A quorum must be present for meetings to proceed.
7.5 Decisions are made by vote, with the chairperson having a deciding vote in the event of a tie.
7.6 Minutes of all meetings must be kept and made available to members.
7.7 Sub-committees may be formed as needed and must report back to the management committee regularly.

**8. Annual General Meetings**
8.1 The annual general meeting must be held annually towards the end of the financial year.
8.2 The AGM agenda will include:
- Approval of agenda items
- Attendance and apologies
- Reading and confirmation of previous minutes
- Chairperson’s report
- Treasurer’s report
- Any proposed changes to the constitution
- Election of new office bearers
- General business
- Closure of the meeting

**9. Finance**
9.1 An accounting officer shall be appointed at the AGM to audit the organisation’s finances.
9.2 The treasurer will manage day-to-day finances, ensure funds are deposited in the organisation’s bank account, and maintain proper financial records.
9.3 Withdrawals from the bank account require signatures from the chairperson and at least two other members.
9.4 The financial year ends on March 31 each year.
9.5 Financial records and reports must be submitted to the Director of Nonprofit Organisations within six months after the financial year-end.
9.6 Any investable funds will be invested with registered financial institutions or in securities listed on a licensed stock exchange.

**10. Changes to the Constitution**
10.1 Changes to the constitution require a resolution passed by at least two-thirds of members present at an AGM or special meeting.
10.2 Two-thirds of members must be present to change the constitution. Notice of proposed changes must be given 14 days prior to the meeting.
10.3 No amendments may result in the dissolution of the organisation.

**11. Dissolution/Winding-Up**
11.1 The organisation may be dissolved by a resolution passed by at least two-thirds of members present at a meeting convened for that purpose.
11.2 Upon dissolution, all debts must be paid. Remaining assets should be given to another nonprofit organisation with similar objectives.
