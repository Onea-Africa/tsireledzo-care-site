About Tsireledzo Care

Rooted in empathy, committed to change.

Headed in the Thulamela region of Limpopo, this female-led organisation exists to address bullying among learners in school settings by providing social work interventions and psychosocial support. It also supports learners identified as bullying perpetrators with life skills education that builds conflict resolution, critical thinking, self-awareness, and problem solving.

Our mission To protect learners from harm while helping young people build the skills and confidence to participate in professional life.

Objectives

A dual-impact ecosystem for safety and opportunity.

01

Anti-Bullying & Psychosocial Support

Address bullying among learners through school-based social work interventions, psychosocial support, emotional restoration, and practical support for vulnerable learners.

02

Life Skills Education

Educate learners identified as bullying perpetrators and guide them toward socially acceptable behaviors through conflict resolution and self-awareness.

03

Youth Employment Pathways

Collaborate with technology companies to insource local youth into projects, especially in the fiber space industry.

Constitution

Transparent governance for lasting community trust.

Tsireledzo Care is constituted as an organisation with its own legal identity, able to own property, continue beyond changes in membership, and act in its own name.

Management

The female-led organisation is anchored by Tsireledzo as main member, with Takadzani supporting financial accountability and Livhuwani strengthening social work support. Its committee structure follows the constitution's requirements for office bearers and re-election.

Income and Property

Money and property may not be distributed to members or office bearers except as reasonable payment for work performed or reimbursement for expenses.

Finance

Financial records are maintained by the treasurer, audited by an accounting officer, and submitted after the March 31 financial year-end.

Dissolution

If the organisation winds up, debts are paid first and remaining assets go to another nonprofit organisation with similar objectives.
